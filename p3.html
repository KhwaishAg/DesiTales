History

India’s history spans over 5,000 years, beginning with the Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE), one of the world’s earliest urban cultures, known for advanced city planning, drainage systems, and trade networks. The Vedic Period (1500–500 BCE) saw the rise of Hinduism and the composition of the Vedas, shaping Indian culture and philosophy. The Maurya Empire (321–185 BCE) united most of India under Chandragupta Maurya and his grandson Ashoka, who promoted Buddhism. The Gupta Empire (319–550 CE) ushered in a golden age of science, mathematics, and literature, with scholars like Aryabhata and Kalidasa. During the medieval period, India witnessed the rise of Rajput kingdoms, the Delhi Sultanate (1206–1526), and the Mughal Empire (1526–1857). The Mughals, especially Akbar, brought administrative reforms, Persian influences, and architectural marvels like the Taj Mahal. By the 18th century, the British East India Company took control, leading to British colonization (1858–1947).

Diwali

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of India’s most significant festivals. Celebrated by millions, it symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. People decorate their homes with diyas (oil lamps), rangoli, and lights, exchange sweets, and burst fireworks. It marks the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana. Goddess Lakshmi, the deity of wealth, is worshipped for prosperity. Families gather for prayers, feasts, and celebrations.

Holi

Holi, the Festival of Colors, is a joyful celebration that marks the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. It is rooted in Hindu mythology, particularly the legend of Prahlad and Holika, symbolizing the triumph of devotion over arrogance. Holi is widely celebrated across India, with Mathura, Vrindavan, and Barsana hosting grand events. The festival promotes love, joy, and unity, breaking social barriers. It is also observed in Nepal and by Indian communities worldwide, making it a truly universal festival of happiness and color.
